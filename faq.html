Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common IT support questions

General Support

You can submit a support request through our Support Request Logging Form, via email at ithelp@techsupport.com, or through our Live Chat system. Make sure to include your name, department, computer name, and a detailed description of the issue.

GSC Tech Support operates 24/7/365. Our team is always available to assist with critical issues. Response times vary based on priority level as outlined in our Service Level Agreement.

Resolution times depend on the complexity and priority of your issue. Critical issues receive immediate attention with a 1-hour response time. Standard issues typically receive a response within 4 hours. Check our SLA page for detailed response and resolution times.

Yes! You can view all pending and resolved tickets on our Ticket Management page. Each ticket has a unique ID and shows its current status (New, In Progress, Resolved, or Closed).

Hardware & Equipment

Computers older than 3 years or showing declining performance are eligible for replacement. Submit a request through our support form, and our team will evaluate based on the Asset Register data. Replacements are prioritized based on age, incident frequency, and business impact.

First, check that the power cable is securely connected and the power outlet is working. Try a different outlet if available. If the issue persists, submit an urgent support request immediately. Refer to our Troubleshooting Guide for systematic diagnostic steps.

Yes, hardware upgrade requests can be submitted through the support form. Include justification for the upgrade (specific software requirements, performance issues, etc.). Upgrades are evaluated based on business need and budget availability.

Software & Applications

Software installation requests must go through IT for security and licensing compliance. Submit a request specifying the software name, version, and business justification. Our team will evaluate, approve, and install approved software on your system.

Document when the crashes occur (specific actions, time of day, error messages). Submit a support ticket with this information. Our team will investigate potential causes including software conflicts, insufficient resources, or corrupted installations.

Critical security updates are applied immediately. Regular updates are scheduled during maintenance windows (typically after business hours). You'll receive notifications before major updates that may require system restarts.

Network & Connectivity

Check that your network cable is securely connected (for wired connections) or that WiFi is enabled (for wireless). Restart your computer. If the issue persists, contact support immediately as network connectivity is considered a high-priority issue.

Slow connections can result from network congestion, background updates, or local computer issues. Close unnecessary applications and browser tabs. If the problem persists across multiple users, it may be a network-wide issue that requires immediate attention.

Remote access requires VPN credentials and proper authorization. Contact IT support to set up remote access. You'll receive secure login credentials and instructions for connecting to company resources from external locations.

Security & Passwords

Password resets can be requested through the support form or by contacting IT directly. For security purposes, you'll need to verify your identity. Passwords must meet complexity requirements (minimum 8 characters, including uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters).

Do NOT click any links or download attachments from suspicious emails. Forward the email to IT security immediately and delete it from your inbox. Never provide passwords or sensitive information via email, even if the request appears to come from IT.

Company policy requires password changes every 90 days. You'll receive reminders before your password expires. Use unique passwords for different systems and never share your credentials with anyone, including IT staff.
